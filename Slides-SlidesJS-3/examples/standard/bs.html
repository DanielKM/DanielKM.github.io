Wilfrid Laurier University

Bachelor of Science

I started my biology career by volunteering in my sister's lab during her first year of her PHD. It was a fun group of intelligent people and the work was for a good cause: to research the impacts of climate change on bugs! I started out as a poor student at WLU, but through hard work I quickly turned myself into someone others would go to for help. I achieved excellent grades, and completed an undergraduate thesis on herbivorous mites. The most important thing I learned in my undergraduate was the ability to adapt and learn new things quickly, even if they at first seem impossible.

Master of Science in Biology

During my master's I studied the impacts of water quality on wetland vegetation. My research showed that phosphorus levels prevalent in southern Ontario waters are capable of impacting plant life, even if the phosphorus levels are beneath detection limits of common instrumentation. The greatest skill I acquired from my studies was the ability to troubleshoot anything, from complicated software to peristaltic pumps. I also learned the importance of collaboration and maintaining excellent relationships with colleagues and supervisors.
